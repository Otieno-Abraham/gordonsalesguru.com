When clients first reach out, many are weighing custom kitchen cabinets against the cheaper, ready-made units you find in big stores. It's a fair question — why pay more? Having delivered kitchens across Kenya for years, here's my honest take on where custom genuinely earns its keep.

## A perfect fit, every time

Off-the-shelf cabinets come in fixed sizes. Real kitchens don't. You end up with awkward filler panels, wasted corners and gaps that collect dust and never look quite right. Custom cabinets are built to your exact measurements, so every centimetre works for you — including those tricky corners and that narrow run beside the fridge.

## Storage designed around *your* life

This is the part clients fall in love with. With custom cabinetry, we design the inside, not just the outside: deep drawers for pots, a pull-out for spices, a dedicated bin cupboard, charging drawers, appliance garages. Your kitchen ends up working the way you actually cook, not the way a factory guessed you might.

## Built for Kenya's conditions

Cheap flat-pack units often use low-grade board that swells with humidity and edges that peel within a couple of years. I specify moisture-resistant materials, proper edge-banding and quality hinges and runners that stand up to daily use and our climate. A custom kitchen isn't just prettier — it lasts.

## The finish you can't fake

Premium finishes, consistent colour, soft-close everything, and doors that line up perfectly — these are the details that make a kitchen feel expensive. Mass-produced units rarely get them all right. When everything is made and installed to spec, the result simply looks and feels better.

## The real cost comparison

Yes, custom costs more upfront. But factor in the lifespan. A well-made custom kitchen can last 15–20 years and still look smart. A budget kitchen often needs replacing in five to seven. Spread over time, custom frequently works out cheaper — and you enjoy a far better kitchen the whole way through.

## It adds value to your home

In Nairobi's property market, the kitchen sells the house. A beautifully fitted, durable kitchen is one of the strongest selling points you can offer, and buyers notice quality immediately.
