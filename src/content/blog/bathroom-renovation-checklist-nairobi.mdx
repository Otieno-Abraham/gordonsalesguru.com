A bathroom renovation runs far more smoothly when you've planned it properly. This is the checklist I walk Nairobi homeowners through before we lift a single tile — it saves money, prevents nasty surprises, and gets you a bathroom you'll love.

## 1. Define how you'll use it

Is this a busy family bathroom, a serene master en-suite, or a guest bathroom that needs to impress? The answer shapes everything — storage needs, whether you want a double vanity, and how hard-wearing the finishes need to be. Start here.

## 2. Set a realistic budget

Decide what you're comfortable spending and where it should go. The biggest cost driver is moving plumbing, so keeping fixtures roughly in place stretches your budget further. Prioritise the vanity and lighting — the things you use and see most.

## 3. Plan the layout

Map out where the basin, toilet, shower and storage will go, keeping in mind clearances so the room feels comfortable to move around. A wall-hung vanity frees up floor space and makes a compact bathroom feel larger. This is the stage to get right before anything is ordered.

## 4. Choose moisture-proof materials

This is non-negotiable in Nairobi's humidity. Specify moisture-resistant vanity boards, quality edge-banding, and hardware that won't rust. Cutting corners here means redoing the bathroom in a few years.

## 5. Get the storage right

Plan generous, hidden storage so nothing clutters the surfaces — drawers that work around the plumbing, a tall cabinet or a mirrored cabinet for everyday items. A clutter-free bathroom always feels more luxurious.

## 6. Layer the lighting

Don't rely on a single ceiling light. Plan warm LED at the mirror for grooming, ambient light for atmosphere, and ideally a glow beneath a floating vanity. Lighting is one of the cheapest ways to make a bathroom feel high-end.

## 7. Pick a cohesive palette

Choose your tiles, vanity finish and tap metal together as one palette. Consistency — a single tap finish carried throughout, a calm colour scheme — reads as far more expensive than a mix of pricey but clashing elements.

## 8. Plan the sequence and timeline

Renovations have an order: strip-out, plumbing and electrics, tiling, then fitting the vanity and fixtures. Knowing the sequence and a realistic timeline keeps the project on track and your household sane.

## 9. Confirm what's included

Make sure your quote spells out everything — vanity, basin, taps, mirror, removal of the old fittings — so there are no surprise costs midway.
