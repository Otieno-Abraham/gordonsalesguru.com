A children's wardrobe has a tough brief: it needs to be safe, easy for little hands to use, packed with storage, and — crucially — able to adapt as your child grows. With a bit of clever design, one well-made wardrobe can serve a child from toddler years right through to their teens. Here's how I approach it for Kenyan families.

## Design it to grow

The biggest mistake is building a wardrobe for the child your son or daughter is now, rather than the one they'll become. I design children's wardrobes with **adjustable shelves and rails** so the layout can change over the years — low rails and lots of shelving for toddlers, taller hanging space and a desk-height drawer unit for teens. One wardrobe, many phases.

## Keep daily items within reach

Young children gain confidence and independence when they can reach their own clothes. I place everyday items — school uniforms, play clothes — on low rails and accessible shelves, while keeping higher sections for items only a parent needs, like spare bedding or out-of-season clothes.

## Safety first

For kids' rooms, safety is non-negotiable. That means soft-close hinges and runners so little fingers don't get caught, rounded design where it matters, and secure fixing to the wall so nothing can tip. These are standard in everything I build, but they matter even more in a child's room.

## Maximise storage for the chaos

Children come with a lot of stuff — clothes, shoes, toys, school bags, sports kit. I build in generous, varied storage: deep drawers for bulky items, open cubbies for bags and shoes, and a dedicated spot for the school bag so mornings run smoother. A place for everything genuinely helps kids stay tidy.

## Make it theirs

A child's wardrobe can be fun without being something they'll outgrow in a year. I favour a clean, neutral base that can be personalised with handles, a feature colour or accessories — easy to update as tastes change, without rebuilding the whole thing.

## Build for shared rooms

Many Kenyan families share children's rooms between siblings. A fitted wardrobe can be divided into clear, equal zones so each child has their own space — reducing squabbles and keeping things organised.

## Built to last through it all

Kids are hard on furniture, and our climate is hard on cheap materials. I use moisture-resistant boards and durable hardware so the wardrobe survives years of energetic use.

## Designed around your child

Every child and every room is different. I'll design a wardrobe that fits your space, your family and the years ahead.
