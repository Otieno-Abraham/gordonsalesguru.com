Once we've designed your wardrobe's interior, there's one more big decision: the doors. Sliding or hinged? Both look fantastic when done well, but they behave very differently in a real bedroom. Here's how I help Nairobi clients choose.

## Sliding doors: the space-savers

Sliding doors don't swing outward, which makes them perfect for tight bedrooms or where furniture sits close to the wardrobe. There's no clearance to worry about, so you can place a bed or dresser right up against them. They also create a sleek, seamless wall of storage that looks effortlessly modern.

The trade-off: you can only ever access part of the wardrobe at a time, because one panel always sits behind another. And the running track needs occasional cleaning to keep the glide smooth.

## Hinged doors: full access, classic look

Hinged doors open the whole wardrobe at once, so you see everything in a single glance — easier for browsing your clothes and reaching corners. They suit a more classic aesthetic and let you add interior fittings to the back of the doors, like mirrors or accessory racks.

The catch is space: they need room to swing open, so they're less suited to cramped bedrooms or spots near a bed.

## Style considerations

Sliding doors shine with large, calm panels — matte finishes, soft wood tones, or a full-height mirror that doubles your light and space. Hinged doors offer more variety of look, from shaker-style detailing to handleless minimalism, and feel more like fine furniture.

## Cost and maintenance

Sliding systems rely on quality tracks and rollers; cheap mechanisms jam and rattle, so this is not the place to economise. Hinged doors use simpler, well-proven hardware but need quality soft-close hinges to feel premium. Both last well when the hardware is good — which is exactly why I never fit bargain-bin fittings.

## My rule of thumb

For compact Nairobi apartments and bedrooms where space is tight, sliding doors usually win — especially with a mirrored panel. For larger bedrooms where you want to see your whole wardrobe at once and love a more classic look, hinged doors are hard to beat.
