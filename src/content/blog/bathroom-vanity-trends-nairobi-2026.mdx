The bathroom has quietly become one of the most design-forward rooms in the Nairobi home, and the vanity is its centrepiece. It's where storage, style and daily routine meet. Here are the five vanity trends I'm fitting most often in 2026 — and how to make them work in your space.

## 1. Floating (wall-hung) vanities

Mounting the vanity off the floor is the single biggest trend right now. It makes the bathroom feel larger, the floor easier to clean, and the whole room more contemporary. In compact Nairobi bathrooms, that visual breathing room is gold. Add a strip of LED light underneath and it almost seems to hover.

## 2. Natural wood and warm tones

The all-white bathroom is giving way to warmth. Wood-grain finishes, soft taupes and warm neutrals are everywhere, paired with brass or matte-black taps. The look is spa-like and inviting rather than clinical — perfect for unwinding after a long Nairobi commute.

## 3. Smart, generous storage

Clients are done with cluttered countertops. Modern vanities hide everything: deep drawers with cutouts for plumbing, pull-outs for bottles, and dedicated spots for hairdryers and toiletries. Good storage is the difference between a bathroom that photographs well and one that actually stays tidy.

## 4. Statement basins

Countertop and semi-recessed basins are having a moment. A beautiful basin sitting on a wood or stone-effect top instantly elevates the room. I help clients balance the look with practicality — some statement basins are harder to clean around, so the choice matters.

## 5. Double vanities in the master

For couples, the his-and-hers double vanity is increasingly a must-have in master bathrooms. Two basins, two sets of drawers, and no more morning queue. Where space is tight, a wide single basin with a double-width counter gives a similar feel.

## Making trends work for *your* bathroom

Trends are a starting point, not a rulebook. The right vanity for you depends on your bathroom's size, your plumbing layout and how many people use it. A floating vanity is stunning, but it needs solid wall fixing; a double vanity is luxurious, but only if the room can take it without feeling cramped.
