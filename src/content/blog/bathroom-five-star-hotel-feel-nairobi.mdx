You know that feeling when you walk into a great hotel bathroom — calm, clutter-free, everything considered? You can absolutely have it at home. A five-star bathroom isn't about size or extravagance; it's about a handful of details done well. Here's how I create that hotel feeling for Nairobi clients.

## Start with a clutter-free vanity

Hotel bathrooms feel serene because nothing is sitting out. The foundation is a vanity with enough hidden storage to swallow every bottle, brush and gadget. A wall-hung vanity with deep, well-organised drawers keeps surfaces clear — and a clear surface is the single biggest "luxury" signal there is.

## Get the lighting right

Hotels layer their lighting: soft ambient light for atmosphere, bright even light at the mirror for grooming, and often a warm glow beneath the vanity. Replacing a single harsh ceiling light with warm, layered LED lighting instantly transforms the mood from functional to indulgent.

## Choose a calm, cohesive palette

Five-star bathrooms rarely shout. They use a restrained palette — warm neutrals, natural textures, one metal finish carried consistently through taps and fittings. Cohesion is what reads as expensive. Pick your tap finish (brass and matte black are both timeless) and repeat it everywhere.

## Add a statement mirror

A large, well-lit mirror is a hotel staple. It bounces light, makes the room feel bigger, and becomes a feature in itself. A backlit mirror or a generously sized framed one immediately lifts the space.

## Texture and warmth

Wood-grain vanity finishes, stone-effect surfaces and quality fittings bring the natural warmth that defines modern luxury bathrooms. These textures stop a clean bathroom feeling cold or clinical.

## The finishing touches

It's the small things that complete the hotel feel: a niche in the shower for bottles so nothing sits on the floor, a heated rail or a dedicated spot for fresh towels, and a place for everything to be put away. When the practical details are handled invisibly, the room feels effortless.

## Build it to survive the steam

A hotel bathroom that warps doesn't stay luxurious. In Nairobi's humidity, I only use moisture-resistant materials and quality hardware, so your spa retreat still looks pristine years from now.

## It's about design, not budget

You don't need a huge budget — you need the right choices in the right order. I'll help you design a bathroom that feels like a getaway every single morning.
