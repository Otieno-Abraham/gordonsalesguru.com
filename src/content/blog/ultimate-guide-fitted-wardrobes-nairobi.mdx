A fitted wardrobe is one of the best investments you can make in a Nairobi bedroom. Unlike a free-standing wardrobe that wastes space and never quite fits, a fitted wardrobe uses every centimetre and is built around exactly what you own. Here's my complete guide to getting it right.

## Why fitted beats free-standing

Free-standing wardrobes leave dead space above and beside them, wobble over time, and rarely match the room. Fitted wardrobes run floor-to-ceiling and wall-to-wall, turning an ordinary bedroom into one with serious, seamless storage. They also look like part of the architecture rather than a piece of furniture dropped in.

## Plan the interior first

The magic of a fitted wardrobe is on the inside. Before choosing doors, I work out the right mix of:

- **Hanging space** — full-height for dresses and coats, double-hang for shirts and trousers to double capacity.
- **Shelving** — adjustable shelves for folded items and bags.
- **Drawers** — for underwear, accessories and anything you'd rather not have on display.
- **Shoe storage** — angled racks or dedicated shelves keep pairs visible and tidy.

The goal is a place for everything you own, sized to your actual wardrobe.

## Choosing your doors

Doors set the style and affect how the room functions:

**Sliding doors** are ideal for tight Nairobi bedrooms because they don't swing into the room. They look sleek and modern, especially with mirror or matte panels.

**Hinged doors** give you full access to the whole interior at once and a more classic look. They need clearance to open, so they suit larger rooms.

## Finishes that suit the room

Light wood tones and soft neutrals keep bedrooms calm and feel timeless. A mirrored sliding door doubles as a full-length mirror and bounces light around — a clever trick in smaller apartments. For a premium master bedroom, a handleless design in a warm matte finish looks effortlessly high-end.

## Lighting makes the difference

Built-in LED strips along shelves and rails transform a wardrobe from functional to luxurious. They also make it far easier to actually find what you're looking for in the early-morning dark.

## Built for our climate

I always specify moisture-resistant boards and quality runners and hinges. Nairobi's humidity is hard on cheap materials, and a wardrobe is something you want to last well over a decade.
