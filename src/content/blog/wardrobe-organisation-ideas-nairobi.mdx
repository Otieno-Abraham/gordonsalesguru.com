A wardrobe that's beautiful on the outside but chaotic on the inside helps no one. The real value of a custom wardrobe is in how it's organised — and that's something I design around your actual life. Here are the organisation ideas that genuinely work for Nairobi homes.

## Start by zoning

Great wardrobes are divided into clear zones: long-hang for dresses and coats, double-hang for shirts and folded-over trousers, shelves for knitwear and bags, and drawers for the small stuff. When everything has a defined home, getting dressed becomes effortless and your clothes last longer because they're not crushed together.

## Double your hanging space

This is the simplest win there is. Most shirts, blouses and folded trousers only need about a metre of vertical space, so stacking two rails doubles your capacity in the same footprint. I split each wardrobe into long-hang and double-hang sections based on what you actually own.

## Drawers earn their keep

Open shelves look great in photos but collect clutter in real life. Drawers keep underwear, socks, accessories and folded tees tidy and out of sight. Internal drawers within a wardrobe also feel more premium and protect delicate items from dust — a real consideration in dusty seasons.

## Dedicated accessory and shoe storage

Belts, ties, watches, sunglasses and jewellery disappear into the bottom of bags without proper homes. Shallow accessory drawers with dividers solve this instantly. For shoes, angled racks or adjustable shelves keep pairs visible so you actually wear them rather than forgetting they exist.

## Lighting changes everything

Built-in LED strips on rails and shelves make it easy to see colours and find items, especially in the early morning before sunrise. It's a small touch that makes a wardrobe feel genuinely luxurious and far more usable.

## Plan for the seasons

Nairobi's weather means a mix of light and warm clothing year-round, plus the occasional formal outfit. I design in higher, harder-to-reach storage for off-season or rarely used items, keeping your daily pieces at eye and arm level.

## Pull-outs and clever extras

Pull-out trouser racks, valet rods for laying out tomorrow's outfit, and hampers built into the wardrobe all reduce daily friction. These small additions are what separate a good wardrobe from one you love.

## It all comes back to your habits

The best organisation isn't generic — it's built around how *you* dress and what *you* own. When I design a wardrobe, I ask exactly those questions first, then build the interior to match.
