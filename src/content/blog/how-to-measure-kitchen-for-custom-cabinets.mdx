Accurate measurements are the foundation of a custom kitchen — get them wrong and even the finest cabinets won't fit. While I always take final measurements myself before production, it helps to understand the process and to have rough numbers when you first get in touch. Here's a simple step-by-step.

## What you'll need

A steel tape measure (not cloth), a pencil, and a notebook or a printed sketch of your kitchen. Work in millimetres for accuracy — centimetres round too much. Take your time and double-check each figure.

## Step 1: Sketch the room

Draw a rough plan of your kitchen from above, marking each wall, the door, the window and where water and power come in. It doesn't need to be neat — it just needs to show the layout so your measurements have a home.

## Step 2: Measure wall lengths

Measure each wall from corner to corner, at the height where the cabinets will sit. Record every wall, even short ones. Walls are rarely perfectly straight or square, so this matters more than people expect.

## Step 3: Measure ceiling height

Measure floor to ceiling in a few places — heights often vary across a room. This determines how tall the units and any bulkhead can be, and whether floor-to-ceiling storage is possible.

## Step 4: Mark services and obstacles

Note the exact position of the sink waste, water inlets, gas point, electrical sockets and the extractor outlet. Also mark anything immovable — a meter box, a structural column, a radiator. These dictate where certain units must go.

## Step 5: Measure doors and windows

Record the width and height of windows and doors, and how far they sit from the corners. You need to know how far a window sill is off the floor too, since it affects worktop and unit placement.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **Forgetting the skirting** — it affects how units sit against the wall.
- **Assuming corners are 90°** — they almost never are.
- **Measuring once** — always measure twice and write it down immediately.
- **Ignoring clearances** — appliance doors and drawers need room to open.
