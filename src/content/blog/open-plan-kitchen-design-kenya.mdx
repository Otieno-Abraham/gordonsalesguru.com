Open-plan living is hugely popular in new Nairobi homes and apartments — and for good reason. It feels spacious, sociable and modern. But an open kitchen also has to work harder, because it's always on show and connected to where you relax. Here's what genuinely works in Kenyan homes.

## The island is the anchor

In an open plan, the island does a lot of work. It defines the kitchen zone without walls, offers extra prep space and storage, and gives people somewhere to perch and chat while you cook. I design islands with practical storage on the kitchen side and often a clean overhang for stools on the living side.

## Manage the cooking smells

This is the question I'm asked most. With the kitchen open to the living and dining areas, ventilation matters. A good extractor over the hob is essential — not optional — to keep cooking aromas from settling into sofas and curtains. I always plan for proper extraction from the start.

## Hide the mess

Because the kitchen is visible from everywhere, clutter has nowhere to hide. The answer is generous closed storage and clever features: a tall larder, an appliance garage to tuck away the kettle and toaster, and deep drawers that swallow everything. A pantry cupboard or a small utility area for the noisy, messy jobs keeps the main kitchen serene.

## Tie it to the living space

An open kitchen should feel like part of the room, not a separate zone bolted on. I choose cabinet finishes and colours that complement your flooring and living-room palette, so the eye flows smoothly from one space to the next. Often that means slightly more furniture-like finishes than you'd use in a closed kitchen.

## Think about noise and flow

Hard surfaces bounce sound around, so open plans can get echoey. Soft-close hardware keeps the kitchen quiet, and thoughtful layout keeps the cook out of the main walkway. I plan the work triangle — sink, hob, fridge — so cooking is efficient and you're not crossing the living space constantly.

## Lighting sets the mood

Open plans benefit from layered lighting: bright task lighting over work areas and softer, warmer lighting for when the kitchen becomes part of an evening with guests. Under-cabinet LEDs and a feature light over the island do this beautifully.

## Designed around your home

Every open plan is different, shaped by your room's proportions, your windows and how you live. That's exactly what a tailored design accounts for.
