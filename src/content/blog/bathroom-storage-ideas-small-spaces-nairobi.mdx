In a small Nairobi bathroom, storage is everything. Get it right and the room feels calm, spacious and easy to keep clean. Get it wrong and you're forever shuffling bottles around a cluttered counter. Here are the storage ideas that genuinely transform compact bathrooms.

## Build storage into the vanity

The vanity is your storage workhorse. Instead of doors that hide a single awkward shelf, I build **deep drawers that work around the plumbing**, reclaiming space most stock units waste. Drawers let you see and reach everything, and they keep the contents organised rather than piled in a dark cupboard.

## Go vertical

When floor space is tight, look up. Tall, narrow cabinets use otherwise dead vertical space and hold a surprising amount. The wall above the toilet is prime real estate for a slim cabinet or shelving — storage that takes up no usable floor at all.

## Use a mirror cabinet

A mirror cabinet is a small-bathroom hero: it gives you a mirror and concealed storage in the same footprint, perfect for medicines, skincare and daily essentials. It keeps these items off the counter and out of sight while drawing the eye upward, which makes the room feel taller.

## Create a shower niche

A recessed niche built into the shower wall is one of my favourite touches. It gives shampoos and soaps a permanent home, so nothing clutters the floor or perches precariously on the edge of the bath. It looks intentional and high-end, and it costs little when planned in early.

## Float the vanity

A wall-hung vanity not only looks modern — the clear floor beneath makes the room feel bigger and gives you a spot for a basket or stool if needed. The visual space is as valuable as the physical storage in a tight bathroom.

## Keep surfaces clear

The golden rule of small-bathroom design: if it's on the counter, the room feels small. The whole storage strategy should aim at empty surfaces. When everything has a dedicated, hidden home, even a tiny bathroom feels serene and considered.

## Make every element earn its place

In a compact space, there's no room for storage that doesn't pull its weight. Custom design means each drawer, shelf and niche is sized and placed for exactly what you own — no wasted volume.

## Built for the damp

All this storage needs to handle steam and splashes, so I use moisture-resistant materials throughout. Beautiful storage that warps isn't a solution at all.
