"How much will my kitchen cost?" It's the question on everyone's mind, and the honest answer is: it depends — but not in a vague, unhelpful way. Let me walk you through exactly what drives the price, so you can budget with confidence. (You won't find specific figures here; I quote every kitchen individually because every kitchen is different.)

## What actually drives the cost

**Size and layout.** More cabinetry means more materials and labour. A galley kitchen costs less than a large U-shaped kitchen with an island, simply because there's less of it.

**Materials.** The board, the door fronts, the worktop and the finish all sit on a spectrum. Moisture-resistant boards and premium finishes cost more than basic options — but they last far longer, which matters in our climate.

**Hardware.** Soft-close hinges, full-extension drawers and quality runners add cost, but they're what make a kitchen feel premium every day. This is rarely the place to cut corners.

## Where people overspend — and underspend

I often see clients pour money into a showpiece worktop while skimping on the drawers they'll open ten times a day. My advice is the opposite: invest first in the things you touch constantly — runners, hinges, storage — then choose finishes that fit what's left. A balanced kitchen feels better than an unbalanced expensive one.

## The hidden costs to plan for

Worktops, splashbacks, sinks, taps and appliances are easy to forget when budgeting for "the kitchen." So is the cost of removing and disposing of your old units. A good quote spells all of this out clearly, so there are no surprises halfway through.

## How to get the most for your budget

1. **Be honest about your budget early.** It lets me design something achievable rather than something we have to strip back later.
2. **Prioritise.** Tell me what matters most — storage, looks, durability — and I'll allocate accordingly.
3. **Think long-term.** Spending a little more on quality now usually saves you a full replacement in a few years.
