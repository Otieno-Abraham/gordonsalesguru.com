A luxury bathroom isn't about spending the most money — it's about spending it in the right places. I've helped many clients in Kenya achieve a genuinely high-end bathroom without an unlimited budget. The secret is knowing where to invest and where to hold back. Here's how.

## Spend on the vanity

The vanity is the heart of the bathroom and the thing you use every day, so this is where your budget works hardest. A well-built, wall-hung vanity with quality drawers and a beautiful finish instantly lifts the whole room. Even a small, perfectly made vanity reads as luxurious — whereas a cheap one drags everything down with it.

## Save on the layout

The most expensive part of any bathroom renovation is moving plumbing. If you keep your basin, toilet and shower roughly where they already are, you save significantly — money you can then redirect into finishes that you actually see and touch. Reworking the layout should only happen when it genuinely improves how the room functions.

## Let lighting do heavy lifting

Good lighting is one of the cheapest ways to make a bathroom feel five-star. Warm LED around the mirror, a strip beneath a floating vanity, and a single well-placed feature light create atmosphere that expensive tiles alone never will. It's a small spend with an outsized effect.

## Choose finishes that punch above their price

You don't need rare stone to look premium. A consistent palette, a statement tap in brass or matte black, a large frameless mirror, and clean, well-fitted tiling deliver a luxury feel at a sensible cost. Cohesion looks more expensive than a pile of pricey but mismatched elements.

## Prioritise storage

Nothing kills a luxury feel faster than clutter on every surface. Building generous, hidden storage into the vanity keeps the room serene. This is where a custom vanity beats an off-the-shelf one — it's designed around your space and your plumbing, wasting nothing.

## Don't compromise on moisture resistance

In Kenya's climate, bathrooms take a humidity beating. Spending a little on moisture-resistant materials protects everything else you've invested in. A "luxury" bathroom that warps in two years isn't luxury at all.
