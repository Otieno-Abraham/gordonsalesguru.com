Apartment bathrooms in Nairobi are often compact — but compact doesn't have to mean cramped or cluttered. With the right vanity and a few smart design choices, even the smallest bathroom can feel calm, spacious and genuinely luxurious. Here's how I approach it.

## Go wall-hung to win back floor space

The single most effective trick for a small bathroom is a floating, wall-hung vanity. By lifting it off the floor, you reveal more tiling underneath, which fools the eye into seeing a bigger room. It's also far easier to clean around — no awkward gaps collecting dust.

## Choose the right basin

In a tight bathroom, a semi-recessed or compact countertop basin gives you usable counter space without eating into the room. I help clients pick a basin that leaves somewhere to actually put a soap dish and a toothbrush — practicality matters as much as looks.

## Storage that disappears

Clutter is what makes a small bathroom feel small. The solution is storage that hides everything:

- **Drawers over doors** — drawers reach the full depth of the vanity and keep contents organised.
- **Plumbing cutouts** — custom drawers that fit around the pipes reclaim space most stock units waste.
- **A tall mirror cabinet** — storage and a mirror in one, drawing the eye upward.

When everything has a home, the room instantly feels bigger.

## Use light and mirrors

A large mirror — or a mirrored cabinet — bounces light around and visually doubles the space. Pair it with good lighting (warm LED around or behind the mirror) and the whole bathroom lifts. Light vanity finishes help too; they reflect rather than absorb light.

## Keep the palette simple

Busy patterns and lots of contrasting colours shrink a small room. A clean, consistent palette — light vanity, simple tiles, one accent in the tapware — keeps things serene and spacious. Save the bold statement for one small feature, like a brass tap or a textured basin.

## Make it last in our climate

Small bathrooms get steamy fast, so moisture resistance is non-negotiable. I only fit boards and finishes built to handle humidity, so your vanity still looks new after years of daily showers.

## Big results from a tailored design

The reason these bathrooms work is that nothing is off-the-shelf — the vanity is built to the exact dimensions of your room, around your exact plumbing. That's how you get maximum storage and style from minimum space.
