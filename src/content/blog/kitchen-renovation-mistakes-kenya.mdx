A kitchen renovation is exciting — but it's also where I see the most avoidable, expensive mistakes. Over the years I've been called in to fix plenty of them. Here are the five biggest, and exactly how to steer clear.

## 1. Skipping the planning stage

The most common mistake is rushing into buying cabinets before properly planning the layout. People fall for a look online and try to force it into a room it doesn't suit. The result is a kitchen that's awkward to cook in. Always start with how you use the space — the work triangle of sink, hob and fridge — and design around that before choosing anything pretty.

## 2. Choosing cheap materials and hardware

In a bid to save money, many people pick the cheapest board and basic hinges. In Kenya's humidity, low-grade board swells and edges peel within a couple of years, and cheap runners sag and stick. You end up replacing the kitchen far sooner than you should. Spend wisely on moisture-resistant materials and quality soft-close hardware — it's cheaper in the long run.

## 3. Underestimating storage

People consistently underestimate how much storage they need, then end up with cluttered counters. The fix is designing the *interior* of every cabinet — deep drawers, pull-outs, a tall larder, a dedicated bin cupboard — so everything has a home. Good storage is what keeps a kitchen looking as good as the day it was installed.

## 4. Ignoring ventilation and lighting

A beautiful kitchen with poor extraction fills the home with cooking smells, and one with a single harsh ceiling light is unpleasant to work in. Plan proper extraction over the hob and layered lighting — task lighting under cabinets plus warm ambient light — from the very beginning, not as an afterthought.

## 5. Hiring on price alone

Choosing the cheapest quote almost always costs more eventually. Corners get cut on materials, measurements or installation, and you're left with gaps, misaligned doors and no after-sales support. Choose someone who measures properly, quotes transparently, installs with their own team, and stands behind the work.

## The common thread

Every one of these mistakes comes back to planning and quality. A renovation done right the first time saves money, stress and a second renovation a few years later.
