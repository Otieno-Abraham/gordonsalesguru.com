Layout is the foundation of a great kitchen — get it right and everything else falls into place. Two layouts dominate Nairobi homes: the L-shape and the U-shape. Each has clear strengths. Here's how to know which one suits your space and the way you cook.

## The L-shaped kitchen

An L-shape runs cabinets along two adjoining walls, forming an L. It's flexible, opens easily into a dining or living area, and works beautifully in open-plan homes and apartments. It leaves room for a table or a small island and keeps the kitchen feeling airy.

**Best for:** open-plan spaces, apartments, and anyone who wants the kitchen to flow into the rest of the home. It's also more forgiving in smaller rooms.

**Watch out for:** in a large L-shape, the work triangle can get stretched, meaning more walking between sink, hob and fridge.

## The U-shaped kitchen

A U-shape wraps cabinets around three walls, surrounding the cook. It offers the most storage and counter space of any layout and keeps everything within easy reach — ideal for serious cooking.

**Best for:** dedicated kitchen rooms and households that cook a lot and need maximum storage and worktop.

**Watch out for:** it needs enough width so the room doesn't feel boxed in, and it's less suited to fully open-plan layouts because of the third wall.

## Workflow: the deciding factor

Think about the work triangle. The U-shape keeps the triangle tight and efficient — great if you cook daily. The L-shape spreads things out more, which suits casual cooking and sociable, open spaces where you want people around you.

## Storage comparison

The U-shape wins on sheer capacity thanks to that third run of cabinets, including the option of two corner units. The L-shape offers slightly less but, with smart interiors — pull-outs, deep drawers, a corner carousel — it can hold far more than people expect.

## Can you add an island?

Both layouts can take an island if the room is big enough, turning an L into a very sociable kitchen or giving a U-shape extra prep space. An island also adds storage and a casual seating spot.

## My recommendation

For open-plan Nairobi apartments and homes, I most often recommend an L-shape, sometimes with an island. For a separate kitchen where cooking is a priority, the U-shape is hard to beat. But the truth is, the right layout depends on your exact room.
