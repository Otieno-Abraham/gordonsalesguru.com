It's one of the most common questions I get from apartment owners in Nairobi: should I go for a walk-in wardrobe or a built-in one? Both can be stunning and both can be custom-made to fit perfectly. The right answer comes down to your space, your storage needs and how you want your bedroom to feel.

## Built-in wardrobes: the space-savers

A built-in (or fitted) wardrobe sits flush against the wall, using every centimetre from floor to ceiling. For the typical Nairobi apartment — where bedroom space is precious — this is usually the smart choice. It keeps the floor clear, the room feeling open, and the storage hidden behind clean doors.

Built-ins are also brilliant for awkward spaces: sloped ceilings, alcoves, that tricky corner beside the window. Because they're made to measure, nothing is wasted.

## Walk-in wardrobes: the dream upgrade

A walk-in wardrobe is a small room of its own — open shelving, hanging rails, drawers and sometimes a dressing area. It feels luxurious, keeps everything visible, and turns getting ready into a pleasure rather than a scramble.

The catch is space. You typically need a spare 4–6 square metres, which in many apartments means borrowing from the bedroom or a box room. If you have it, though, a walk-in adds real wow factor.

## Comparing the two

**Space:** Built-in wins for small apartments; walk-in needs a dedicated footprint.

**Cost:** Built-ins are generally more budget-friendly because they use less material and hardware. Walk-ins cost more but deliver a premium experience.

**Storage:** Both can be maximised with custom interiors — the difference is whether your clothes are behind doors or on open display.

**Resale value:** Smart, well-built storage adds value either way. In compact apartments, a beautifully fitted built-in often appeals to more buyers.

## My honest recommendation

For most Nairobi apartments, I steer clients toward a floor-to-ceiling built-in wardrobe with a custom interior — adjustable shelves, dedicated shoe and accessory storage, and good lighting. It delivers 90% of the walk-in experience at a fraction of the space and cost.

If you have a larger home or a spare room, and you love the idea of an open dressing space, a walk-in is absolutely worth it.
