When it comes to cabinet doors, two styles dominate Nairobi kitchens: the classic shaker and the sleek flat panel. Both are excellent — the right one depends on your taste, your home and how you live. Here's a clear comparison to help you choose.

## What is a shaker door?

A shaker door has a simple recessed centre panel framed by four flat edges. That subtle frame gives it a timeless, slightly traditional character that works in almost any home — from a classic Karen house to a modern apartment. It's the most versatile door style there is, which is why it never really goes out of fashion.

## What is a flat panel door?

A flat panel (or slab) door is exactly what it sounds like — a single, smooth, flat surface with no frame or detailing. It's the definition of modern and minimal, especially when paired with handleless push-to-open mechanisms. It suits contemporary, open-plan spaces beautifully.

## Looks and atmosphere

Shaker brings warmth and a sense of craftsmanship; it feels homely and considered. Flat panel feels crisp, architectural and uncluttered. If you love a cosy, classic or transitional look, shaker wins. If you want sleek and ultra-modern, flat panel is your style.

## Cleaning and practicality

This is a real, everyday difference. Flat panel doors have no grooves, so they wipe clean in seconds — ideal for busy kitchens with lots of cooking. Shaker doors have that recessed frame, which can catch a little dust and grease and needs slightly more attention to keep spotless. Not a dealbreaker, but worth knowing.

## Cost

Flat panel doors are generally a touch more economical because they're simpler to make. Shaker involves a little more work in the framing. The difference is modest, and both can be specified at a premium level with quality materials and hardware.

## Which suits Nairobi homes?

Honestly, both are popular and both look superb here. I tend to suggest:

- **Shaker** for homes with a classic or transitional feel, and for anyone who wants a look that will still feel right in fifteen years.
- **Flat panel** for modern apartments and open-plan spaces where a clean, seamless look is the goal — and for households that want the easiest possible cleaning.

## You can even mix them

Sometimes the best answer is both — shaker on the main run for warmth, flat panel on an island for a modern edge. Tailored design lets us combine styles tastefully.
