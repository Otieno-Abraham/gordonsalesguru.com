What to Ask Before Buying Kitchen Cabinets in Nairobi

The essential questions to ask before buying kitchen cabinets in Nairobi — on materials, hardware, installation, timelines and after-sales — so you buy with confidence.

By Gordon Odhiambo 24 June 2025 4 min read

Buying a kitchen is a significant decision, and the right questions up front will save you from expensive regrets later. Whoever you choose to work with, here are the questions I'd want you to ask — because a confident, transparent answer is the sign of someone you can trust.

"What materials are you actually using?"

Not all boards are equal. Ask specifically about moisture resistance, the quality of edge-banding, and the thickness of the carcass. In Nairobi's climate, cheap board swells and edges peel within a couple of years. You want a clear, honest answer — not a vague "it's good quality."

"What hardware comes as standard?"

Hinges and drawer runners are what you'll touch every day for years. Ask whether soft-close hinges and full-extension runners are included or cost extra. Quality hardware is the difference between a kitchen that still feels premium in a decade and one that's rattling within months.

"Is this made to measure or standard sizes?"

This matters enormously. Standard-size units leave filler gaps and waste corners. Made-to-measure cabinets fit your room exactly. If you're paying custom prices, make sure you're actually getting custom work.

"Who installs it, and is installation included?"

A beautiful kitchen badly installed is a disappointment. Ask who does the installation, whether it's included in the price, and what happens if something doesn't fit. With me, installation is handled by my own team and managed personally — I don't hand you over to strangers.

"What's the realistic timeline?"

Get a clear answer on how long from approved design to installed kitchen. A trustworthy answer accounts for production and shipping, not just an optimistic guess. You should also be told how you'll be kept updated along the way.

"What's included — and what isn't?"

Worktops, splashbacks, sinks, taps, appliances and removal of your old kitchen are often quoted separately. Ask for an itemised quote so you can see exactly what's covered and avoid surprise costs halfway through.

"What after-sales support do you offer?"

Things occasionally need adjusting after installation — a hinge here, a tweak there. Ask what support you get afterwards. A confident supplier stands behind their work; a cheap one disappears once they've been paid.

The right answers build trust

When you ask these questions, you'll quickly tell who's transparent and who's hoping you won't notice the gaps. I'd rather over-explain than have you wonder.
