Double Sink vs Single Sink Bathroom Vanity: What Works in Kenya

Double or single sink bathroom vanity? A practical guide for Kenyan homes on space, cost, plumbing and lifestyle to help you choose the right vanity.

By Gordon Odhiambo 5 July 2025 4 min read

A double vanity feels like the ultimate master-bathroom luxury — but is it right for your space, and is it always worth it? Having fitted both across Kenyan homes, here's an honest comparison to help you decide between a double and a single sink vanity.

The case for a double vanity

Two basins mean no more morning queue. For couples who get ready at the same time, a double vanity genuinely improves daily life — each person has their own basin, their own counter space and their own drawers. It also looks impressive and adds appeal in a master en-suite.

The case for a single vanity

A single basin gives you something a double can't: more usable counter space and more storage. With only one basin and its plumbing, you free up the rest of the vanity for deep drawers and clear surfaces. In a compact bathroom, a single vanity almost always works better.

It comes down to space

This is the deciding factor. A double vanity needs real width — squeeze two basins into too narrow a unit and you end up with two cramped basins and no counter or storage, which serves no one. As a rule, if the wall isn't wide enough to give each basin comfortable space plus some counter, a generous single is the smarter choice.

Plumbing and cost

A double vanity means a second set of plumbing, which adds to both the installation cost and complexity. A single vanity is more economical and simpler to fit. If budget is tight, a wide single basin on a double-width counter gives much of the look of a double at lower cost.

The clever middle ground

For many master bathrooms in Nairobi, I recommend a wide single basin centred on a long vanity, with generous drawers either side. It feels luxurious and spacious, gives both people room, and avoids the storage sacrifice of twin basins. It's often the best of both worlds.

Storage either way

Whichever you choose, the interior is what makes it work — drawers built around the plumbing, dividers for toiletries, and a clear, clutter-free top. A custom vanity makes the most of whatever configuration suits you.

Built for our climate

Both options need moisture-resistant materials and quality hardware to survive Kenya's humidity. That's standard in everything I fit.

Let's size it to your bathroom

The right answer depends entirely on your bathroom's width and how you use it. Tell me your dimensions and I'll recommend the vanity that fits your space and your routine.
