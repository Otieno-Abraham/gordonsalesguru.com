How to Choose the Perfect Kitchen Cabinet Style for Your Nairobi Home

A practical guide to choosing kitchen cabinet styles for Nairobi homes — from shaker to handleless — matched to your space, lifestyle and budget.

By Gordon Odhiambo 13 June 2025 4 min read

Choosing a kitchen cabinet style is one of the most important decisions you'll make when building or renovating a Nairobi home. The cabinets set the tone for the entire room, they're the first thing guests notice, and — because they get used every single day — they have to work as hard as they look good. Here's how I help clients narrow it down.

Start with how you actually live

Before we talk about looks, I always ask one question: how do you use your kitchen? A busy family that cooks every evening needs deep drawers, tough surfaces and easy-clean finishes. A young professional in an apartment might prioritise sleek lines and clever storage over sheer capacity. Your style should follow your lifestyle, not fight it.

The styles worth knowing

Shaker is the timeless choice — a simple framed door that suits both classic and modern Nairobi homes. It's endlessly versatile and ages beautifully.

Flat-panel (slab) doors give you that clean, contemporary look. Paired with handleless push-to-open mechanisms, they create the minimalist kitchens that are so popular in new apartments right now.

Glossy and matte finishes each have their place. Gloss bounces light around smaller kitchens and feels luxurious; matte hides fingerprints and gives a softer, premium feel that's trending hard in East Africa.

Colour and contrast

White and light wood tones remain the safest, most resale-friendly choices for Nairobi homes — they keep kitchens feeling bright and open. But I'm seeing more clients confidently mix two tones: a darker island or base run against lighter wall units. Done well, it adds depth without dating quickly.

Think about the climate

This is where local experience matters. Nairobi's humidity and the coastal climate in Mombasa can be tough on poorly made cabinetry. I only specify moisture-resistant boards, quality edge-banding and hardware that won't rust, so your kitchen still looks new years from now.

Don't forget the hardware

Hinges, runners and handles are the details that separate a cheap kitchen from a premium one. Soft-close everywhere, full-extension drawers, and handles that feel solid in the hand — these are the things you touch daily, and they're worth getting right.

Match the style to your whole home

Your kitchen shouldn't feel like a different house. I always look at your flooring, your living space and the overall feel you're going for, then recommend a cabinet style that ties it all together.
