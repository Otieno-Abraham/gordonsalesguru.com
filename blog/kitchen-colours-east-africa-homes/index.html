Top Kitchen Colours Trending in East African Homes Right Now

The kitchen colours trending across East African homes right now — warm neutrals, two-tone schemes and bold accents — and how to choose one that lasts.

By Gordon Odhiambo 21 June 2025 4 min read

Colour is the quickest way to give a kitchen personality — and the easiest thing to get wrong. The shade that looks incredible online can feel overwhelming across a full run of cabinets. Here's what's trending in East African homes right now, and how to choose a colour you'll still love in ten years.

Warm neutrals are leading

The cool greys that dominated for years are softening into warm neutrals — greige, mushroom, soft taupe and creamy off-whites. They feel inviting rather than clinical, they flatter our warm natural light, and they pair beautifully with wood floors and brass or black hardware. If you want timeless, this is the safest, most elegant route.

Two-tone kitchens

Mixing two colours is one of the strongest trends I'm fitting. Typically that means lighter wall units with a darker island or base run — or natural wood paired with a painted finish. Two-tone adds depth and stops a large kitchen feeling flat, while keeping it from looking dated quickly.

Natural wood is back

Wood-grain finishes are having a real moment, especially in lighter oak-style tones. They bring warmth and texture, work brilliantly in open-plan spaces, and connect the kitchen to the rest of the home. Used as an island or a feature wall of tall units, wood feels both modern and grounded.

Bold accents, used carefully

Deep green, navy and charcoal are popular for clients who want character. My advice: use them as accents, not all over. A green island, a navy pantry, or a charcoal run beneath lighter uppers gives you boldness without the risk of a colour you tire of. The bigger the surface, the safer the colour should be.

Don't forget the worktop and splashback

Cabinet colour never works in isolation. A warm neutral cabinet can read completely differently against a white worktop versus a stone-effect one. I always choose cabinet colour, worktop and splashback together, as a single palette, so the finished kitchen is harmonious rather than a collection of nice-but-clashing choices.

Think about light and resale

Kitchens that get less natural light benefit from lighter, reflective finishes. And if resale is on your mind, neutral, broadly appealing palettes attract more buyers than highly personal colour schemes.

See it before you commit

Colours shift under different lighting, so I always recommend viewing finishes in person and against your own space. Visit the showroom, or send me photos of your kitchen and I'll suggest a palette that suits the room and the light.
